El problema no es el tiempo verbal.  
Es **qué forma exige el verbo que va después**.

---

# 1️⃣ Verb + to + infinitive

**Estructura:**

> verb + to + base verb

**Ejemplos:**

- decide to leave
    
- plan to travel
    
- hope to see
    
- try to improve
    
- want to go
    
- need to study
    

✔ I decided to leave early.  
❌ I decided leaving.

---

# 2️⃣ Verb + -ing

**Estructura:**

> verb + verb-ing

**Verbos comunes:**

- avoid doing
    
- enjoy doing
    
- consider doing
    
- suggest doing
    
- keep doing
    
- mind doing
    
- finish doing
    

✔ I avoid talking about it.  
❌ I avoid to talk.

---

# 3️⃣ Verb + object + to + infinitive

**Estructura:**

> verb + person + to + base verb

**Muy importante (tu punto débil).**

**Verbos clave:**

- want you to
    
- expect him to
    
- need her to
    
- ask them to
    
- tell me to
    
- would like you to
    

✔ I expect you to be honest.  
❌ I expect you will be honest.  
❌ I want that you come.

---

# 4️⃣ Verb + thing + to + person

Algunos verbos NO permiten doble objeto directo.

**Estructura:**

> explain something to someone  
> say something to someone

✔ She explained the problem to me.  
❌ She explained me the problem.

Pero:

✔ She told me the problem.  
("tell" sí permite doble objeto)

---

# 5️⃣ Verb + preposition + -ing

Algunos verbos necesitan preposición fija.

**Estructura:**

> verb + preposition + -ing

**Ejemplos:**

- insist on doing
    
- depend on doing
    
- think about doing
    
- succeed in doing
    
- believe in doing
    

✔ She insisted on paying.  
❌ She insisted to pay.

---

# 6️⃣ Verb que cambian significado (-ing vs to)

## Stop

- stop smoking → dejar de fumar
    
- stop to smoke → parar para fumar
    

## Remember

- remember doing → recuerdo haber hecho
    
- remember to do → recordar hacer
    

## Try

- try doing → probar hacer
    
- try to do → intentar hacer
    

Este bloque es C1 funcional real.

---

# 📌 Regla mental rápida

Cuando uses un verbo nuevo, pregúntate:

1. ¿Va con **to**?
    
2. ¿Va con **-ing**?
    
3. ¿Va con **persona + to**?
    
4. ¿Lleva preposición?
    

No improvises la estructura.
