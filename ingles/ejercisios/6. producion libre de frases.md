Objetivo: entrenar **fluidez bajo presión**, que es lo que realmente diferencia B2 de C1 al hablar.

Este ejercicio obliga al cerebro a:

- producir ideas rápido
    
- conectar discurso
    
- usar estructuras naturales automáticamente
    

Duración: **10 minutos**

---

# Regla principal

Debes producir **5 frases seguidas** sin parar.

Estructura obligatoria:

opinion → explanation → contrast → experience → conclusion

---

# Paso 1 — opinión

Empieza con tu opinión.

Patrones típicos que aparecerán:

- I think…
    
- Personally…
    
- To be honest…
    

Ejemplo:

> I think working alone is much better.

---

# Paso 2 — explicación

Explica por qué.

Patrones naturales:

- the thing is
    
- the reason is
    
- that's because
    

Ejemplo:

> The thing is, when you work alone you have more control over your time.

---

# Paso 3 — contraste

Introduce otra perspectiva.

Conectores:

- however
    
- although
    
- at the same time
    

Ejemplo:

> However, some people prefer working in teams.

---

# Paso 4 — experiencia

Cuenta algo que te pasó.

Patrones que aparecen aquí:

- I ended up
    
- what happened was
    
- one thing led to another
    
- it turned out
    

Ejemplo:

> For example, last year I tried working in a team, but one thing led to another and we ended up arguing.

---

# Paso 5 — conclusión

Termina con reflexión.

Patrones:

- that's why
    
- so in the end
    
- which is why
    

Ejemplo:

> So in the end I realized I prefer working on my own.

---

# Ejemplo completo

Tema: vivir en otro país

> I think living in another country changes people a lot.  
> The reason is that you are constantly exposed to new situations.  
> However, not everyone adapts in the same way.  
> For example, when I first moved abroad I struggled a lot with the language.  
> So in the end it made me much more independent.
