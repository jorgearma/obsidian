Regla principal:

> No pensar demasiado la gramática. Hablar primero, corregir después.

---

# Estructura diaria

Duración total: **30 minutos**

1️⃣ Speech largo (10 min)  
  
2️⃣ Corrección  
  
3️⃣ Conversación dinámica (15 min)  
  
4️⃣ Repetición de correcciones (5 min)

---

# 1️⃣ Speech largo (10 minutos)

Habla **2–3 minutos seguidos** sobre un tema.

Escribe o graba lo que dirías.

Objetivo:

- producir mucho lenguaje
    
- conectar ideas
    
- usar estructuras complejas
    

Reglas:

- mínimo **8 frases**
    
- no parar a pensar demasiado
    
- hablar como en una conversación real
    

Ejemplo de temas:

Why I enjoy my current job.  
  
Why freedom at work is important.  
  
Why I want to move into cybersecurity.  
  
What I have learned living in Denmark.  
  
What kind of life I want in the future.  
  
Why many people stay in jobs they hate.

Ejemplo de respuesta:

> I have been working like this for about seven years, and honestly I love it.  
> What I enjoy the most is the freedom that it gives me.  
> If I don't feel like working one day, I can simply decide not to go.  
> That kind of freedom is something that most people never experience.  
> Even though the job is not perfect, it allows me to organize my time.  
> At the same time, I have been studying cybersecurity because eventually I want to change careers.

---

# 2️⃣ Corrección

Analiza los errores.

Formato de corrección:

❌ Error original  
  
✅ Corrección  
  
Explicación breve

Ejemplo:

❌ I have been try to finish this project.  
  
✅ I have been trying to finish this project.  
  
Error:  
Después de "have been" se usa verbo en -ing.

Objetivo:

- detectar errores recurrentes
    
- mejorar precisión
    

---

# 3️⃣ Conversación dinámica 

Conversación real.

Responde preguntas inesperadas.

Objetivo:

- reaccionar rápido
    
- hablar sin preparar
    
- usar estructuras automáticamente
    

Ejemplos de preguntas:

Why do you think many people hate their jobs?  
  
If you had started studying cybersecurity earlier, where would you be now?  
  
Do you think freedom is more important than money?  
  
What have you learned about yourself in the last few years?  
  
What would you do if you suddenly had complete financial freedom?  
  
Why do some people never change their lives?

Reglas:

- responder **4–6 frases mínimo**
    
- no escribir perfecto
    
- simular conversación real
