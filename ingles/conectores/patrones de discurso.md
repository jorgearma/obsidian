# 1. The thing is (that)…

Se usa para introducir la idea principal.

Ejemplo:

The thing is that it's more complicated than it looks.

Español aproximado:

“La cuestión es que…”

---

# 2. What I mean is (that)…

Sirve para aclarar lo que estás diciendo.

Ejemplo:

What I mean is that the problem isn't the language, it's the speed.

---

# 3. The point is (that)…

Se usa para enfatizar la conclusión.

Ejemplo:

The point is that we need more time to finish it.

---

# 4. It depends on…

Muy usado para evitar respuestas absolutas.

Ejemplo:

It depends on the situation.

---

# 5. That's why…

Conectar causa y consecuencia.

Ejemplo:

I didn't sleep well, that's why I'm tired today.

---

# 6. At the end of the day…

Se usa para resumir una idea.

Ejemplo:

At the end of the day, experience matters more than theory.

---

# 7. The problem is that…

Introducir una dificultad.

Ejemplo:

The problem is that we don't have enough information.

---

# 8. On the other hand…

Introducir contraste.

Ejemplo:

Working from home is comfortable. On the other hand, it can be isolating.

---

# 9. At first…, but then…

Narrar cambio de situación.

Ejemplo:

At first I thought it would be easy, but then I realized it was much harder.

---

# 10. The more…, the more…

Comparación progresiva.

Ejemplo:

The more you practice, the more confident you become.
